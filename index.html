Tinysign — Sign Language Recognition in Low-Resolution Settings

Published Research · SIViP 2024

First-author peer-reviewed research: making sign language recognition work when video resolution is too low for existing models.

Problem & Approach

Existing sign language recognition models fail on low-resolution video — exactly the conditions of privacy-sensitive and low-bandwidth scenarios. I introduced the first low-resolution benchmark for the task and proposed a novel pose-conditioned super-resolution architecture that reconstructs the visual cues recognition depends on.

Results

  • 58.0% accuracy vs. 35.0% prior state of the art
  • New public benchmark for low-resolution recognition
  • Published in Signal, Image and Video Processing (Springer)

Fingerprint Segmentation for Biometric Identification

Production · HAVELSAN AFIS

Computer vision model deployed inside a national-scale automated fingerprint identification system.

Problem & Approach

Reliable identification requires cleanly isolating fingerprint regions from noisy scans before matching. I built a PyTorch segmentation model, benchmarked state-of-the-art architectures, and improved robustness with data augmentation and systematic hyperparameter tuning.

Results

  • 92% segmentation accuracy in production
  • Integrated into an automated identification workflow
  • Benchmarked against multiple SOTA architectures
